Adversarially learned inference Adversarially learned inference(ALI) Two distributions Encoder joint distribution \(q(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z})=q(\mathbf{x})q(\mathbf{z}|\mathbf{x})\) Decoder joint distribution \(p(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z})=p(\mathbf{z})p(\mathbf{x}|\mathbf{z})\) Notes Encoder margin : \(q(\mathbf{x})\) is the empirical distribution; Decider margin : \(p(\mathbf{z})\) is the sampler distribution(Maybe Gaussian); Adversarially learned inference(ALI) Objective Matching two joint distributions. Matching game Joint pairs \((\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z})\) are drawn either from \(q(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z})\) or \(p(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{z})\); A discriminator network learns to discriminate between the two; The encoder and decoder networks are trained to fool the discriminator.
